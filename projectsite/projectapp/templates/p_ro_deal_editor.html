Project Details

Context

The Deal Editor in RevOps is a tool to create a standardized Sales Agreement easily. Sales Operations can create “templates” and Sales could then use those templates and edit prices and additional products, but if they exceed a certain threshold (i.e. price change is beyond 10%).

Where we started

Before adopting RevOps our users were working with silo’d programs such as Microsoft Word and Excel – this made it extremely difficult to easily experiment with pricing on-the-fly because none of the changes would be tracked; no one would know who made the pricing change; and it is not obvious that any pricing change were made until you open up the file itself.

The first version of RevOps solved these pains by becoming the source of truth for products/SKUs, pricing, and deal information.

Caption

The Deal Editor is the most-used part of the RevOps platform. To make it enticing to brand new users who may sign up for the service itself, we wanted to explore how we could make the tool’s UX better and even stickier. I made a user journey map for 2 personas – Sales Operations and Sales – to track potential questions, concerns, and hopes a brand new user may have.

I came with 2 goals for the new design:

  1. 1

    How might we make the Deal Editor even more intuitive so that SalesOps doesn’t have to spend time training their teams to use the tool?
  2. 2

    How might we give Sales Reps immediate feedback and context for whether their deal customization will trigger a need for manual approval?

    Since time is of the essence, Sales Reps would like to estimate what criteria would cause a manual approval vs. automatic approval. That way they could craft the best deal possibly given the situation.

The Result

With the goals in mind, I mocked up a new design for the Deal Editor that was a hybrid of inline editing and using a sidepanel. Our users were very familiar with tools like Word and Google Docs, so leveraging preëxisting familiarity was best and most intuitive.

The above video shows the new UI for showing price adjustments and informing the Sales Rep of any needed approvals.

The mock above is an overview of the design improvements we explored: Table of Contents for easy navigation of the sales agreement, and ablity to add comments on specific parts of the deal.

The "Table of Contents" on the left serves as a way for quick navigation to different parts of the deal. It also highlights the parts where required fields may be missing – making if extremely easy for Sales Reps to know what they need to fill out.
